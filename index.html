03
May 9 · Saturday
SLC → Bryce Canyon → Hurricane
6am departure · Early Bryce arrival · Scenic drive + merch + hike
+
🚗
6:00 AM
Depart SLC Airbnb
Early start to beat the Bryce crowds and heat. Grab coffee from Roots Coffee (opens 6:30am, 0.5 mi away) on the way out. I-15 S to US-89 S to UT-12 W. ~2.5 hrs to Bryce.
🚗
8:30 AM
Bryce Canyon — East Rim Scenic Drive
Scenic Drive
Drive the full length of the park road (18 miles one way) from the entrance south to Rainbow Point at 9,115 ft. This drive hits every major overlook — Bryce Point, Inspiration Point, Sunset Point, Sunrise Point, and Natural Bridge. Pull over at each. Dog can stay in AC car for these quick overlook stops.
🐾
9:30 AM
Rim Trail Walk
Dog on Leash
The paved Rim Trail connects Sunset and Sunrise Points — about 1 mile, flat, and totally dog-friendly on leash. Walk it with the pup while you soak in the hoodoo amphitheater views. Best in the morning light. Cool at 8,000 ft elevation.
🛍
10:30 AM
Bryce Canyon Visitor Center + Merch
Stop at the Visitor Center for maps, ranger info, and the gift shop. Great selection of Bryce-specific souvenirs, books, pins, and apparel. One of the better national park stores. Dog can hang in AC car — this is a quick 20-30 min stop.
🏠
~2:00 PM
Arrive Hurricane Airbnb
1052 South 50 West, Hurricane, UT 84737. ~2 hrs from Bryce. Your home base for 5 nights! Arrive in the early afternoon with plenty of time to settle in and explore the neighborhood.
04
May 10 · Sunday
Zion National Park — Full Day
6am departure · East entrance approach · Drive, hike, Pa'rus Trail with pup
+
🚗
6:00 AM
Depart Hurricane → Zion East Entrance
Take US-89 N to UT-9 W — enter Zion from the EAST side. This is the most dramatic approach: you drive through open red rock desert before plunging into the canyon. ~45 min from Hurricane. Arriving early means cooler temps, better light, and emptier parking at trailheads.
🗺
6:45 AM
Checkerboard Mesa — First Stop
Pull-off · 5 min
Right near the east entrance — easy roadside pull-out. The sandstone is covered in a striking crisscross grid pattern that looks like a giant checkerboard. Morning light brings out the texture beautifully. Dog stays in AC car — you're out for 5 minutes max.
🚇
7:00 AM
Zion–Mt. Carmel Tunnel
Drive-Through
Over 1 mile long, carved through solid sandstone in the 1920s. The tunnel has six cut-out windows that frame dramatic views of the canyon — slow down as you pass them. When you emerge on the west side, the canyon floor drops away 800 feet below you. One of the most stunning moments of the whole trip.
🥾
7:15 AM
Canyon Overlook Trail
~1 mile · 45 min · No dog
Trailhead is just past the tunnel on the east side — small parking lot. One of the best bang-for-your-buck hikes in the entire park: 1 mile round trip, moderate, with a panoramic payoff view of Zion Canyon and the Great Arch below. You're actually standing on top of the arch! Dog stays in AC car — well within your 30-min window if you move at a good pace. Go early to snag parking.
🚗
8:15 AM
Drive the Switchbacks into the Valley
After the tunnel, the road descends via six dramatic switchbacks into the canyon floor. Incredible views at every turn. Pull over at the signed viewpoints — there are a few small pull-outs. This section of driving is itself a highlight.
🐾
8:45 AM
Pa'rus Trail — With the Dog
Dog on Leash · Only dog-friendly trail in Zion
Park at the Visitor Center. Pa'rus Trail is one of the ONLY trails in Zion where dogs are allowed. 3.5 miles round trip, paved, flat, follows the Virgin River with massive canyon walls on both sides. Views of The Watchman, West Temple, and the Towers of the Virgin. Do this in the morning cool before the valley heats up.
🛍
10:00 AM
Visitor Center + Forever Project Merch Store
Right at the Visitor Center complex. The Zion Forever Project Store has books, soaps, puzzles, pins, mugs, and apparel. Also check out Trailhead Gifts & Gear just outside the park gates in Springdale (145 Zion Park Blvd) — one of the nicest gift shops around, with a wider variety and no parking hassle.
🚌
10:30 AM
Optional: Shuttle into Zion Canyon
Note: Private cars cannot drive Zion Canyon Scenic Drive in May — shuttle only. If one person wants to ride the free shuttle up the canyon (to see the Court of the Patriarchs, Zion Lodge, Angels Landing viewpoint, Weeping Rock), the other can stay with the pup in the car with AC running. Shuttle runs every 5–10 min from the Visitor Center.
🐾
11:30 AM
George Barker River Park — Springdale
Off-Leash Friendly
Just south of the park entrance in Springdale. Dogs allowed off-leash past the bridge. Great for a final roam before heading back — river access, shade, and stunning canyon backdrop.
🍽
12:30 PM
Lunch in Springdale
Springdale's main street has several great lunch options before heading back to Hurricane. The town is charming with galleries, shops, and good food. Back to Hurricane is just 30–40 min on UT-9 W.

+

Willis Creek Slot Canyon
Dog-Friendly
Near Kanab, ~1 hr from Hurricane. BLM land — dogs fully welcome! Easy 2.5-mile out-and-back through a stunning slot canyon with a creek to hop through. Navigate via Skutumpah Rd ONLY — NOT Adams Wash (dangerous). Go early.

Dogs on Leash
~30 min from Willis Creek. Dogs welcome on trails! The 3-mile Panorama Trail passes spectacular colored spires and rock chimneys. A beautiful complement to Bryce's famous hoodoos, with far fewer crowds. Opens 8am.

Travel with Your Senior Pup

Dog Tips

🌡️
Beat the Heat
Southern Utah in May hits 85–90°F by midday. Aim to be at parks by 7–8am and back to the car by 11am. Keep the AC running whenever pup is in the car.
💧
Hydration
Bring a collapsible bowl and frozen water bottles. Refill at every trail head. Seniors dehydrate faster — offer water every 30 minutes on active days.
🐾
Dog-Friendly Spots
Zion (car drive-through ✓), George Barker River Park (off-leash ✓), Willis Creek (BLM, fully off-leash ✓), Kodachrome Basin (on-leash ✓), Bryce Rim Trail (on-leash ✓), Quail Creek (on-leash ✓), Shoshone Falls (on-leash ✓).
🎟️
Book Permits Now
Zion requires timed entry permits in May — book at recreation.gov ASAP. They sell out. Bryce Canyon also recommends advance reservations for the parking areas.
🚗
Car Safety
Never leave pup in a hot car. If someone needs to stay behind, leave the car running with AC. Crack windows are not enough in Utah summer temps.
🏥
Vet Info
Given your pup's cancer diagnosis, keep vet contacts handy. Nearest options to Hurricane: Staheli Family Vet in Washington City (10 min away) and Zion's Way Animal Hospital in St. George (20 min). For SLC: BluePearl Emergency Vet is open 24/7. Keep any medications easily accessible in the car.
🐾

Safe travels, great sunsets, and happy paws all the way.
